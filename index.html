Plot: in this first book of the series, the 3 siblings become orphans, meet the most wicked man on Earth, risk losing their inheritance and they’re locked up by a hooked man...

My opinion: as the good obsessed reader I am, I was astonished, once I finished “A series of unfortunate events” on Netflix, because I found out it was drawn from a book series I hadn’t read before the show!!! But I guess it’s better this way: of course, every time something exciting was happening I already knew everything, but otherwise the show would have annoyed me because it’s really different from the books. And thank goodness, I’d say. Don’t misunderstand me, I liked the books, but are so much slower than the TV show, they’re much more “for children”: the book series becomes more tangled and mysterious from the fifth book, while the TV one from the second episode (which corresponds to the end of the first book)! Moreover is more mature than the books and it’s really well-done, it adds details to the plot of the books and makes it more complex and stunning; it’s like Daniel Handler wanted to make the saga for a “more adult” public.
And the series answers to many of the questions the last book left unanswered. Not all of them, of course, but in this topic it’s way better than the books (and I’m not the only one who thinks this way). They were unsatisfying, yes, but not only: they put so many expectations in the reader, so many mysteries that had to be solved, details that had to be discovered, allusions that had to be explained and nothing was solved, discovered or explained! I think it would be too much calling them “plot holes”, I think they were programmed. Anyway, it has been frustrating!!!
Also the setting is very odd, with modern technologies and old ones, like a fantasy or a dystopian book but with a plot definitely not fantasy or dystopian (and other congratulations to the Netflix adaptation). The characters, as in a children’s book, have a ridiculous personality which made me laugh so much.
Another odd thing is how the author invented only names with the same first letter: every book, every place, everything has 2 names both starting with the first letter. It must have been hard for the translators (and also with the V.F.D. names).
And what about the wordplays? I loved them! Sometimes at the beginning of a chapter the author was explaining the meaning of an expression and it ended with out of the context (but hilarious) examples and tangled sentences. These were a kind of digression I didn’t want to skip.
Because by the thirteenth book I was sick of the digressions at the beginning of the chapter , I wanted story, and these digressions stopped the story right after something interesting or a plot twist. Why?!?!?!? And what made me even sicker were the digressions at the beginning of the book, where the author summarised the previous books. It’s a thing I hate in every series of books, but in this one were even more boring.
The opposite happened with the explanation of some words: in the first books were explained a word out of 20 (at the beginning it was funny, but then it became annoying), but in the last ones were less and I started to like them. The examples were great! (Moreover I read the last 8 books in English and the explanations were useful).
To be honest, if you can catch the allusions, the books become more “for adults”: there are so many allusions to literature, culture and real people, and the saga isn’t such a childish one anymore. It’s wonderful! Anyway all of them are explained on this website, but I’m proud to say I’ve caught some of them (really few of them, but still).
About the story… it’s fine and intriguing, especially when you get to know more about V.F.D., but it’s totally unsatisfying (after all, the author had warned, hadn’t he?). Fine, he warned, but still... Anyway I found out in “The Beatrice Letters” are narrated a few other adventures of the Baudelaire orphans after the last book and I’m still trying to find a pdf of it because I need to know something else! IT CAN’T END THIS WAY!!! I wrote I liked the TV show best because it has a more complex plot, shorter digressions (and they’re added in an amazing way!), the setting is actually really good and Neil Patrick Harris is the perfect Count Olaf.
I read the first 4 books in Italian, but then we were locked down and libraries were closed, so I read English pdfs of the other 9 books (for this reason I’ll cut off some of the quotes I have, they’re in Italian). I enjoyed very much the original versions because thy mad me learn so many new words and expressions (they’re easy, for children, and all these expressions are explained). To be honest I don’t know how the translators managed to translate some parts as the author wrote them, so I guess I’m happy I read in English.

Spoiler alert: anyway I really liked Klaus. Sometimes I couldn’t stand Violet (even if when I was watching the series I had a crush for the actress who plays her oooops) and Sunny... meh, she’s cute but that’s all. Klaus was always so awkward and looked for libraries everywhere... :) .
Even if Count Olaf beats them all. Neil Patrick Harris as Count Olaf beats them all twice. I laughed a lot. (Don’t make me think about the 01×03 and the 01×04 when Count Olaf dressed up as Stephano or I’ll laugh until I die.)
I missed the Quagmire triplets so much! I wanted to know about them so badly... Isadora’s poetry!!! Quigley and Violet!!! And the useless one.
